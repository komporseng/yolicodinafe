Sarah Stern, a budding artist from Neptune City, vanished on December 2, 2016. Her car was found atop the Route 35 bridge between Belmar and Neptune, leading to marine and land searches in the weeks and months following her disappearance.

Her body never surfaced. The case garnered widespread attention as it shed light on the sinister plot devised by Stern’s former high school classmates, McAtasney and Preston Taylor.

The pair planned to kill Stern for the money she had recently discovered and stage her car on the bridge to make it look like she committed suicide.

Their plans were initially brought to light by Anthony Curry, a former Neptune High School classmate and horror filmmaker. He revealed details of a conversation McAtasney had with him on Thanksgiving 2016.

Curry disclosed that McAtasney had shared his plans to kill Stern and rob her of the large sum of money she had recently found. At the time, Curry did not take the conversation seriously until Stern went missing.

Investigators got Curry to tape a conversation with McAtasney about Stern secretly. Curry met with McAtasney and recorded him talking about the murder for 30 minutes. McAtasney and Taylor were arrested the next day.
