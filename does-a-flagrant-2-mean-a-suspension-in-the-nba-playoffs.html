What is a Flagrant 2 Foul?

A Flagrant 2 foul in the NBA is a designation given to a foul that involves excessive or unnecessary contact, whether the contact is deemed intentional or not. This type of foul is considered more severe than a Flagrant 1 foul and is often characterized by violent or dangerous contact.

Criteria for a Flagrant 2 Foul

The NBA rulebook outlines specific criteria that determine whether a foul qualifies as a Flagrant 2. These criteria typically include:

  • The severity of the contact: If the contact is deemed severe and unnecessary, it increases the likelihood of the foul being classified as a Flagrant 2.
  • The potential for injury: If the contact has the potential to cause serious injury to the player involved, it may be deemed a Flagrant 2 foul.
  • The referee's judgment: Ultimately, the referees have the authority to assess the nature of the foul and determine whether it meets the criteria for a Flagrant 2.

Consequences of a Flagrant 2 Foul in the NBA Playoffs

When a player is charged with a Flagrant 2 foul in the NBA playoffs, several consequences come into play, including:

  • Immediate Ejection: The player committing the Flagrant 2 foul is ejected from the game, leaving their team at a numerical disadvantage for the remainder of the match.
  • Potential Suspension: Unlike in the regular season, where a Flagrant 2 foul may or may not result in a suspension depending on the accumulation of points, a Flagrant 2 foul in the playoffs often carries an automatic suspension for the following game.
  • Impact on Momentum: Beyond the individual consequences, a Flagrant 2 foul can significantly shift the momentum of a game and potentially alter the course of a series.

Review Process for Flagrant 2 Fouls
